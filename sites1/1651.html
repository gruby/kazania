Fear paralyses our potential.
Fear hinders and handicaps our lives.
Fear causes our hope to shrivel and die.
Fear increases the size of our enemy mentally and fear changes our focus
from the promise of God to the present circumstance.
When we harbour fear, when we give place to fear, we are actually giving place to the devil.

The promise of Jesus Christ is not that He would keep us from those troubles

The promise of God is that He will give us enough grace to stand and resist those troubles.
So today, we must learn to get above our fears even when it seems impossible to get above our troubles.
We must deal with fear before fear deals with us.

FREEDOM FROM FEAR

when the trouble seems to surround them,

"Don't be frightened because fear is a thief of faith and hope."
Instead of magnifying the causes of our fears,
we ought to equip ourselves with the thoughts of God, His sovereignty, His invisible world.
If you were to see the other side, you would know that our God has made adequate provision
for us to stand the forces of the adversary.
The reaction of Prophet Elisha is instructive to every born-again Christian today
and worthy of emulation.
Whilst his servant was hot, fearful and flustered, Elisha was cool, calm and confident
because he knew the situation at hand was not a personal battle but one against his God.
He saw the trouble, he saw the armies surrounding the city,
he heard the horses as they approached,
he felt the ground quaking, shivering as the chariots drew closer.
He saw this trouble but his heart was not troubled.
While Elisha's servant was overwhelmed by fear, Elisha overcame his fear.
" 'Don't be afraid,' the prophet answered.
'Those who are with us are more than those who are with them.' "
Prophet Elisha and his servants saw the same army but their reactions were different.
Just like David and the Israelites saw the same giant, Goliath, but their reactions were different.
