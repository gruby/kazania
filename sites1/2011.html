My name is Kofi Smiles.
I am from Accra, Ghana.
In 2009 I suddenly got sick.
I couldn't walk.
I couldn't do anything on my own.
I went to the Korle-Bu Hospital.
I had the best surgeon who attended to me,
yet I still couldn't walk.
The problem that brought me to The SCOAN was that I was diagnosed with advanced rheumatoid arthritis.
The doctor operated on me once
and he injected some chemicals into my knee, hoping that I would be able to walk.
But thereafter, I still couldn't walk.
The problem affected my life tremendously.
I couldn't go to work.
I couldn't go out.
I was inbound bound,

I couldn't do anything on my own.
I used a wheelchair and crutches.
