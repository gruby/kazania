GOOD MORNING & WIN TODAY
TB Joshua
QUESTION: There is a controversy surrounding the ‘Prosperity Gospel’ all over the world
and this has seemed to dent the image of Christianity.
Man of God, what is your view?
ANSWER: The issue is not about prosperity per se; it is about the ultimate motive of the preacher.
John 10:35 says, “If He called them gods, to whom the word of God came
(and the Scripture cannot be broken).”
As God’s Word says in Romans 14:5 and 23, each man must be convinced in his own mind
because anything that does not come from faith is sin.
One of the greatest hindrances to carrying out God’s work
is the tradition inherited by Christians about giving,
whereby we have been taught to give and to expect nothing in return.
When we look at this closely,
we will see that it appears to be exactly opposite to God’s law of sowing and reaping.
Oftentimes, I hear Christians say, “Oh, I give because I want to give; I expect nothing in return,”
instead of saying, “I give because God wants me to give.”
To be sure, many charitable organisations around the world are not Christian
but they give as well as help and care for others.
Suppose a farmer said, “I sow in my fields each spring
because I just love to plant seeds but I expect no harvest.”
How could he continue farming?
Of course, the good farmer expects a good harvest!
Good seeds always produce returns in incredible multiples!
It is God’s will for you to reap more than you sow.
The point is that you must keep your eyes on God, not on the means He might use.
He and He alone is the Source of your expectations.
It is certainly more productive to give than to receive,
since only what one gives is multiplied back to him, not what he receives.
You cannot afford just to give your money because you get a blessing out of giving.
Your giving must be productive.
If your giving does not produce a return of more than you give,
how will evangelism be financed tomorrow?
In 1 Kings 17:11-16, Elijah directed the Widow of Zarephath to bake him a cake first.
This seemed cruel and heartless but when she obeyed,
it was like a farmer planting his choice seed – because it was God who had spoken.
That last cake became her seed cake!
It produced an abundant return of much more than she gave to Elijah.
Her barrel wasted not nor did her cruse of oil fail.
When you give, you must receive more,
as Jesus said, in good measure, pressed down, shaken together and running over!
That is, an abundant return.
God’s pact of plenty cannot fail. It is as sure as John 3:16.
TT: Mel Flowers 28/09/2019
