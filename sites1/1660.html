The miracle was so perfect, so complete that all who once knew this man as a poor man,
as a blind beggar, could hardly believe it was the same man
because of the great changes that had taken place in the life of this man.
Since that man had an appointment with Jesus, his life was turned around.
There was a turnaround in everything that had to do with him.
Jesus is the same yesterday, today and forever.

Jesus knows you better than you know yourself.
Man looks at the outside but Jesus looks at the inside.
It is one thing to see you outwardly; it is another thing to see your faith.
Jesus looks at the heart because faith is of man's heart.
Jesus demands for our faith.
When you are face-to-face with Him, He is not interested in your outward appearance -
your attire, your position - but in your faith
because that is what makes you one with Him.
No matter your situation today, no matter what brought you here today -
show your faith as someone who wants to come to Jesus.
Say, "Neighbour, show your faith as someone who wants to come to Jesus".
In similar encounters with those who Jesus healed, saved, blessed and delivered,
Jesus first asked them probing questions:
"What do you want Me to do for you today?"
"Do you believe in the Son of man?"
"Do you want to get well?"
This means before you show your faith to Him,
you must know what You want Him to be for you.
Say, "Neighbour,
before you show your faith,
you must know what you need God to be for you today."
You need to discover yourself first.
When you discover your weakness, you will discover your need for God's strength.
When someone discovers himself as in need of redemption and salvation,
then he will discover his need for the Saviour
because God is whom you need Him to be.
Let someone say, "God is whom you need Him to be."
What do you need God to be for you today?
Do you want Him to be the God you rely on in times of difficulties and trials?
He shall be.
Do you want Him to be the God who saves and rescues?
Then trust in Him alone.
Do you want Him to be the God that answers prayers?
Then exercise your faith in Him, in His name.
Say, "Neighbour, act faith, no matter what you are going through."
Act faith, as the woman with the issue of blood did.
She was in dire need of healing, but she knew...
When you know how reliable God Almighty is, faith comes spontaneously.
Say, "Neighbour, when you know how reliable God is, faith comes spontaneously".
She heard about Jesus; she looked for Him and found Him.
When she heard about Jesus of Nazareth,
the Bible says, she believed and she spoke to herself.
In Matthew 9:21, she says, "I know, I am convinced.
If only I can touch the hem of His garment, I know I shall be healed."
"I know; if only I can step my feet on this arena of liberty, I will be healed."
Do you believe that?
Let that run into your heart, for Jesus listens to your heart.
She says if only I can touch, get to Him...
What we say, what we do expresses our belief.
She expressed her belief by reaching out to Jesus despite the obstacles.
The Bible says Jesus was surrounded by a very thick crowd - no place to move,
but she kept her focus on Him and she elbowed her way to Jesus and touched the hem of His garment.
The Bible says the power flowed and touched her
and the issue of blood ceased instantly.
I don't know whether you have the same issue today -
with the same attitude of mind, that flow will cease today, in Jesus' name.
What we do expresses our belief.
She reached out to Jesus and Jesus changed her life.
What is your situation?
Are you in the dark - be it in your home, business, career?
Are you looking for the way out?
Ask Saul and he will tell you that in Christ Jesus
there is a glorious light, a light that shuns every shade of darkness in our lives.
Saul, being a Pharisee, knew the Book of Moses but he still did not know who Jesus is.
When you know the truth, where the true light comes from,
you will know whom you are and whom God is.
Say, "Neighbour,
when you discover the truth, where the true light comes from,
you will know whom you are and whom God is."
That is the foundation.
Are you facing a difficult situation, a hopeless situation?
No one is hopeless whose hope is in Jesus Christ.
Ask the man at the pool of Bethesda and he will tell you that
no one is hopeless whose hope is in Jesus.
The Bible says for 38 years the man sought the face of God for his healing.
Miracles were happening by the pool while he lay down hopelessly.
He was in dire need of someone to help him, to rescue him.
He tried everything to help himself by human wisdom but he failed.
But the Scripture says the longer he stayed at the pool,
the more his desire for that Someone, that Helper, until he met Jesus.
Jesus met him at that point of desperation and changed his life.
He asked him, "Do you want to get well?"
The question Jesus asked the man was enough for the man to dismiss Jesus,
but the Bible says his mind was disciplined by the patience he went through.
That gave him the maturity of mind to assess the sincerity and commitment
of the One who was talking to him - Jesus, the One who was able to put an end to his past.
When Jesus said to him, "Take up your mat and walk,"
he obeyed and he was healed.
Say, "Neighbour,
no matter your situation, all you need is an appointment with Jesus."
Do you feel as if life has cheated you?
Ask the woman of Samaria; Jesus met her.
The Bible says she represents all those who are in dire need of redemption
from the cares and lures, entanglements of this world.
She was frustrated but deep inside of her,
she yearned for the radical transformation of life that
only the promised Messiah can bring.
When Jesus revealed Himself to her as the promised Messiah,
the Bible says, she believed and became part of the world of truth.
Today, if you believe, no matter how long you have been in that situation,
your life will never be the same.
What can we learn from these Biblical characters we mentioned?

By divine appointment with Jesus today, there is appointment in disappointment,
there is breakthrough in hardship and there is healing in sickness.
They all came from different backgrounds, but one thing you need to know:
Jesus never rejects anyone who comes to Him by faith and in faith.
The only command of Jesus is: Only believe.

If you believe, all things are possible.
If you believe today, there will be a radical transformation of your life.
Only believe.

Believing in Christ redeems our way.
Believing in Christ Jesus redeems our time.

Just believe and your life will never be the same!
