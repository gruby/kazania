Praise the Lord!
Let someone say, "I am moving higher because I am walking with the Lord".
Give thanks to Jesus for that.
Give thanks to Him.
Say, "Lord Jesus, we thank You for every moment of Your attention."
We are in Your presence, undeserving of Your grace.
Let Your favour and Your mercy speak for us.
Say, "Viewers, you are welcome, in Jesus' name."
Have your seats in His presence.
Thank You, Jesus.
Hallelujah!
How are you today?
You are fine?
Thank You, Jesus.
You are blessed, you are fine because of Jesus.
Brethren, we are called to put on Christ as our righteousness.
Remind you neighbour, say, "Neighbour, you are called to put on Christ as your righteousness."
But sometimes, particularly when we are young, we believe we can go it all alone
without any reference to God.
Little do we realise that man may toil and labour hard
but it is the duty of God to determine the time and season of harvest.
Say, "Neighbour, what is your plan?"
When we are making plans for the future without any reference to God Almighty,
when you make plans for tomorrow without any reference to God,
we are guilty of the sin of arrogance and self-sufficiency.
What do I mean?
The unique temptation of a Christian can be spelled out in five words:
fear, ego, pride, greed and moral compromise.
The Scripture says that all high places are slippery places.
When we are prideful, we grieve the Holy Spirit and hurt Him
because we have chosen to follow the suggestion of the adversary, the suggestion of satan.
Satan always suggests to do what is wrong.
That is why as we grow in power, as we grow in influence, in success,
these temptations increase in our behaviour, in our character.
But I have good news for you.
Say, "Neighbour, there is hope".
Say, "Neighbour, if you make a mistake - as we all do - don't run from God, run to Him."
The Bible says when our weakness is exposed,
when imperfection hits, it is our duty to choose the path of faith
and humility and run to God, who is merciful to rescue us,
to sustain us and to get us back up.
This leads us to our message this morning:
THE PATH OF FAITH AND HUMILITY
Say, "Neighbour, follow the path of faith and humility".
No matter what comes, no matter what happens, do not be derailed.
Follow the path of faith and humility.
I believe today the way out for you has come.
Let me take you to the Book of Matthew 7:13.
Jesus said:
"Enter by the narrow gate; for wide is the gate and broad is the way
that leads to destruction, and there are many who go in by it.
Because narrow is the gate and difficult is the way which leads to life,
and there are few who find it."
Say, "Neighbour, you are called to follow Jesus".
To follow Jesus is to follow the way of the Cross
and Jesus warned us that the way is narrow.
Why is the way too narrow?
When you are concentrated on how you are feeling,
when you are concentrated on how you are doing,
how you are treated by people, you cannot go that way.
When we are too much of ourselves, we can't go that narrow way.
Pride and arrogance cannot go that way.
Let someone say, "The way has been made plain.
Neighbour, follow the path of faith and humility."
Pride is a sin in us by nature.
We must be cautioned and armed against it, for a man can truly hide his condition,
his true character, until his pride is injured.
That is why Jesus said in the Book of Luke 9:23
that if you must follow Jesus, you must deny yourself.
You must deny yourself and follow Him.
Remember, God is love and love is humble.
Love does not express itself in arrogance, in pride.
Love is not self-seeking.
So, if you do not deny yourself, you cannot follow Jesus.
Say, "Neighbour, follow the way of the Cross."
No matter what happens in your home, in your business,
no matter your circumstance, follow the way of the Cross.
Jesus is the way to salvation, Jesus is the way to peace, to fulfilment.
Salvation is a total and complete commitment to Him.
We are no longer ourselves - we belong to Him.
You cannot follow Jesus without making a commitment to Him.
When we are committed to Him, asking Jesus to purchase us,
we become Christ's property and we become aliens on earth.
Say, "I am Jesus' property. I belong to Jesus."
No one can snatch me from His hand.
Jesus said something to you and me - that if we do not make a commitment,
we cannot follow Him.
In Matthew 10:38-39, the Lord says to you and me,
"Take up your cross daily, deny yourself and follow Me.
Even if it takes you to be killed for your belief in Christ, if you don't deny yourself,
if you don't take up your cross, you are not worthy of Me."
Ask your neighbour, "Are you a true follower of Jesus?"
What does it take?
In John 6:54 Jesus spoke to those who followed Him,
the crowds that thronged around Him, and said to them,
"If anyone eats My flesh and drinks My Blood,
he has eternal life and I will raise him on the last day."
Upon hearing the words of Christ, many turned their backs
on Jesus and abandoned Him.
Many left Jesus because they were much around miracles and healings,
rather than being around Jesus - the Head of the Church, the Healer Himself.
Judas Iscariot betrayed Jesus
because he was much around Jesus in body but his heart was far from Jesus.
Say, "Neighbour, get rid of pride and embrace God's wisdom."
No matter what you are passing through, don't fight.
Follow the path of faith, humility and self-denial.
That is the key.
Let's look at the lives of biblical characters who followed the path of faith and humility.
Are you a ruler, a king or a president?
Remember Nebuchadnezzar in the Book of Daniel 4:30.
The Bible says that the Babylonian King was overwhelmed by his glory.
One day, seeing the greatness of his empire, he boasted in himself.
He allocated all the glory to his own power and might.
Little did he know that he was just a toy in the hands of God,
who used him as an instrument of judgement to the nations he subdued.
When he honoured himself above the Lord,
instant judgement came upon him in Daniel 4:32-38.
All that he counted dear was stripped away from him:
his kingdom, his glory, his power, his honour, his mental health were taken away from him.
The king became mentally sick and became mad
and lived isolated in the bush for seven years like an animal.
When we do things in pride and arrogance, we honour ourselves and dishonour God;
thus we magnify ourselves above the Lord.
Humbled by his situation, he got to his senses and acknowledged the power
and sovereignty of God, who gives grace to whom He gives grace
and mercy to whom He gives mercy.
And God, who is abounding in mercy, forgave him, restored him back to glory.
What lesson can we learn from him?
When we admit our weakness before God,
all pride and arrogance will be taken away.
When everything that gives pride in us is taken away, humility comes.
After being humbled by his situation, Nebuchadnezzar embraced God's wisdom
and got rid of pride and began to rule in righteousness.
The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom.
What is your situation today?
Run to God.
What is your trouble?
What is pressing you down?
Follow the path of faith and humility because we are undeserving of His grace.
Let's remember the woman from Canaan in the Book of Matthew 15:21 to the end.
The Scripture says that faith is our movement towards God
and faith is not an isolated spiritual force.
Faith works in conjunction with other spiritual forces: love, humility, patience, perseverance.
When she came to Jesus, Jesus referred to the woman as a dog in order to test her faith.
Jesus deliberately provoked her sensibility and sensitivity in order to injure her pride.
But rather than being offended when Jesus equated her to a dog, she said to the Lord,
"Yes, Lord, You are right. I know I am undeserving of what You serve on the table.
Even the little dogs eat their master's crumbs that fall from the table."
Jesus looked into her heart and saw humility and faith and her request was granted.
To honour God, we must come to His presence in sincerity and humility of heart
and acknowledge our position before Jesus as undeserving of His grace.
To acknowledge our position before God is to admit our weakness,
our shortcomings and limitations.
And when we do, the way out has come.
Say, "Neighbour, the way out has come!"
Are you a believer?
Remember what happened to Paul.
The Scripture says that those whom Jesus intends to honour, He first humbles them
in order to prevent them from becoming conceited when the glory,
when the honour eventually comes.
Such was the case of Paul.
He said, "Because of the excellency of revelation of the anointing I carry,
it was given me a thorn in my flesh so I may not fall into pride."
Remember what the man of God said in this year 2020 - his prophecy: This is the year of humility.
Say, "Neighbour, this is the year of humility".
God will humble us with our challenges.
God can use any foolish thing to test our faith.
We must understand that our values must be tested against facts and experiences.
So, what is your thorn?
What is your trouble?
What is your problem today?
That thorn may be to humble you, to strengthen you, to reform you,
to prepare you for the greater heights.
Therefore, don't go out of the way.
Say, "Neighbour, no matter what you are passing through, don't go out of the way".
No matter what you are passing through, don't go out of your duty.
Stay in your home.
Stay in your business.
Stay in your position.
Follow the path of faith and humility.
Finally brethren;
the highest motivation in serving God is the desire to please Him.
And the Bible says, "Only faith pleases God."
As faith and humility honour Jesus the most, so Jesus honours faith and humility the most.
As a Christian, if you see the choice to serve the Lord as your key responsibility in life,
you should guard against unhealthy ego, pride and greed overruling your good intentions.
Say, "Neighbour, follow the way of the Cross.
Follow the path of faith and humility."
How?
The Scripture says we must take our cross and deny ourselves.
This means we must learn to forget ourselves.
We forget ourselves when we choose to do the will of God, rather than our own way.
As a Christian, as a child of God, a daughter of God,
you must choose to do what is right every day,
even though it is contrary to what you naturally want to do.
Are you ready?
Say, "The battle is the Lord's.
The race to survival is not my personal challenge but one with the Lord."
Say, "The battle is the Lord's.
The battle belongs to God."
When we forget ourselves and learn to look to Jesus, humility comes,
for true humility is total dependence on God for everything.
When we forget ourselves, we put off the old man and we put on the new man,
who is renewed in affection, in will, in emotions, and follow the path of faith and humility.
Are you a follower of Jesus?
Say, "Neighbour, are you a follower of Jesus?"
Love your neighbour as yourself.
Are you a follower of Jesus?
Get rid of offence.
Let offence go.
Release forgiveness and the best is yet to come, in Jesus' name.
Thank You, Jesus!
