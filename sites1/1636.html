HOW TO STAY IN THE WILL OF GOD!!! | Racine Sermon (SCOAN)
Let someone say, "Thank You, Jesus."
Say, "Lord Jesus, I thank You for every moment of Your attention."
Here I am, Lord, in Jesus' name.
You may be seated in His presence.
Hallelujah!
How are you today?
The Bible says, all things work for good for those who love God.
In the name of Jesus Christ.
Thank You, Lord.
Jesus is the reason why we are here today.
We know His will for us is to be saved, to be delivered, to be blessed
and to enjoy a life that is full and free.
That life is set before you today.
Say, "Neighbour, the good life is set before you, in the name of Jesus Christ."
The Bible says that, for a sick person to know that it is the will of God to heal him,
it is a seed that must be planted in his heart and in his mind.
For a sinner to know it is God's will to save him,
it is a seed that must be planted in his heart and mind.
Jesus cannot save a sinner unless he knows the will of God on the matter.
Salvation is by faith.
I mean, by trusting the known will of God in the matter.
A man can be sick in body and be a friend of God.
A man can be poor and be a candidate of Heaven.
When He created man, what mattered to God Almighty
was not man's physical or mental disposition but one who would be a stakeholder in God's master plan.
Ask your neighbour, "Are you a stakeholder?"
Say, "Neighbour, yes, I am."
I am a stakeholder in His master plan.
We are created for the glory of God
but for man to glorify God - his Creator - his will must be subject to God's will.
Say, "Neighbour, for you to glorify God, your will must be subject to God's will."
Tell your neighbour, "Let the will of God be done."
Say, "Lord not my will; let Your will be done."
This is our message this morning:
STAY IN THE WILL OF GOD
Tell your neighbor, "Stay in the will of God."
Encourage your neighbour, say, "Neighbour,
whatever you are passing through, stay in the will of God.
Let me take you to the Book of Romans 12.
I start from verse 1.
"I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that you present your bodies
a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable to God, which is your reasonable service.
And do not be conformed to this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind,
that you may prove what is that good and acceptable and perfect will of God."
Let someone say, "Let the will of God be done."
When we realise who we are before God,
we will see the need to always consider the will of God, in whatever we do,
whatever we say and whatever plan we make.
That is why we find it necessary to consult God
to ensure that we are acting and praying according to His will.
His will is to deliver us.
His will is to bless us.
His will is to give you breakthrough today.
His will is to change your life.
That's why you are here.
Say, "That's why I'm here."
In Matthew 7:7-12, it portrays Jesus' invitation to pray
and it is always His will to answer your prayers.
One thing we should know:
God answers the prayers of faith
but He does not always answer it to the letter.
Say, "Neighbour,
though God Almighty answers the prayer of faith,
He does not always answer it to the letter."
That's why today,
when unanswered prayers stand between people and their faith,
some blame God for unfaithfulness.
They fail to realise that
when we pray not according to the Word of God, our prayer will stand upon our lips.
When we pray to God, the answer we receive from Him is determined
but how God chooses to implement His will in the matter.
Say, "Neighbour, let the will of God be done."
The will of God is part of His divine nature.
This means we can pray for it.
"Neighbour, have you said your prayer today?" - ask your neighbour.
Have you forgiven your enemies today?
Prayer is a relationship that exists between God and His children.
God delights in hearing the prayers of His righteous children.
What makes us righteous?
In our prayers we learn to put on Jesus Christ as our righteousness
to have free access to the Father.
Our spiritual union with Jesus establishes a deep sense of intimacy with God, our Father.
That's why the man of God says that prayer is a thing of the Spirit.
Prayer is to the spiritual man what breathing is to the natural man.
Say, "Neighbour,
a break in prayer is a break in faith, and a break in faith is a break with God."
A prayerless home is a Godless home.
Say, "Neighbour, be encouraged; don't let your situation cause you to doubt the will of God."
Pray the more. Fast the more. Stay put. Don't give up!
Whatever you are facing today - it may be lack, you may be in dire need -
but the Scripture says that for a man or woman of faith,
nothing is lacking while everything is necessary.
We are faced by God's uncompromising demand for faith.
God desires faith.
Only faith pleases God.
But today, due to the pressure and tensions of the world, many have abandoned their homes.
Many have abandoned their marriages.
Many have abandoned their businesses.
Many have even given up their faith,
because they think there is no way, no hope, no future.
But let me tell you, there is a way and the way is Jesus!
Say "Neighbour, there is a way for me. The way is Jesus!
Neighbour, stay in the will of God."

Let me encourage you with the life of Paul; we all know Paul.
In the Book of 2 Corinthians 12:7-10,
Paul prayed to the Lord that He may remove the thorn, the pain in his flesh.
He appealed to the Lord three times and Jesus said, "My grace is sufficient for you."
He has learned the secret to be content in whatever situation he faced.
When he knew the will of God on the matter, Paul said, "When I am weak, I am strong.
Your grace is sufficient for me."
Say, "Lord, Your grace is sufficient for me. Let Your will be done".
The word grace means God's will for you and I.
It is sufficient to enlighten us.
It is sufficient to encourage us and to comfort us.
It is sufficient to cheer up our spirit in all distress and affliction of this world.
Let someone say, "His grace is sufficient for you."
Say, "Neighbour, let the will of God be done."
Fear not. Stay put. Stay in the will of God.
It is the Holy Spirit that strengthened and sustained Jesus at the Garden of Gethsemane
and Jesus said, "Father, let Your will be done."
The lesson we learn from Paul: Men and women of faith never give up.
They never abandon their cause, even when the way is so rough.
They stay in the will of God no matter what comes, no matter what happens.
Each day they stay true to their purpose in life through rain and storm.
Whatever the vicissitudes of life, they stay put.
They stay in their marriage, business, waiting for what God has to say; they never give up.
They overcome obstacles daily by keeping their purpose in the forefront of their hearts
because they know that whatever happens to them today,
true fulfilment comes from doing the will of God.
Whatever happens to you, stay put!
To stay put is to stay in the will of God.
Say "Neighbour, stay in the will of God. His grace is sufficient for you."
Today many have changed the course of the history of their lives
through inability to see beyond the situation at hand.
But let me tell you something today and I want you to repeat it with me.
Say, "No mortal man, no devil can supersede the plan of God, the will of God for my life."
No mortal man, no devil can supersede the plan of God for my life.
When you lay hold of this truth, it will set you free.
There is only one person that can get you out of the will of God - it is you.
Say, "Neighbour, there is only one person that can get you out of the will of God - it is you."
We abort the promises of God by our disobedience.
When God created man, He gave him the special right of discretion -
I mean the ability to exercise the will through his mind.
God has given man the power to choose the cause of the action of his life.
Man has the ability to choose, to pray, to teach, and to act
but the Holy Spirit prays, chooses, and does all things according to the perfect will of God.
Say, "Neighbour, stay in the will of God."
How do we know the will of God?
The man of God says we know God by the Word He speaks
and the Word God speaks represents His heart's desire for me.
Say, "Neighbour, I know God by the Word He speaks
and the Word God speaks represents His heart's desire, His will for me."
God speaks to us today through His Word by His Spirit - not to our ears but to our hearts.
We need to allow the Word of God in the midst of our hearts, for there is the will of God.
Say, "Neighbour, stay in the will of God."

Everyone hears through different ears.
Everyone feels through different hearts.
Different hearts means we have different ways of attending to the Word of God.
Some hearts receive the Word of God and others resist the Word of God.

attentively and repeatedly.
