Let someone say, "Son of David, have mercy on me."
Let Your favour and Your divine mercy speak for me.
Say, "Lord, manifest Your strength in my weaknesses."
I thank You for every moment of Your attention, in Jesus Christ's name!
You may be seated.
Welcome in His presence.
Greet your neighbour, say, "Neighbour, welcome to His presence".
Say, "Neighbour, welcome!"
Ask your neighbour, "Are you happy?"
Hallelujah!
Say, "Thank You, Jesus!"
We are happy because Jesus is in our midst.
Thank You, Lord!
Do you know God knows you better than you know yourself?
Say, "My life is in God's hands".
Say, "Neighbour, I am not here by accident. It is the promise of God to give birth to me,
to bless me, to save me, to deliver me.
Thank You, Jesus.
Say, "That's why I'm here".
Thank You, Lord Jesus.
Jesus is the reason we are here this very morning.
Jesus is the reason of our gathering this morning.
The Bible says whether the world laughs or frowns at us, it is an enemy.
That is why the Scripture warns us today.
Don't be too quick to trust.
Find out what God says.
But when we are looking for a leader, when we are looking for a man of God,
for a Christian, for a believer, we are always tempted to look at the externals.
We tend to consider speaking skills - I mean, ability to inspire -
education, genealogy or natural descent.
But the Bible says we must look past appearance to the heart.
Let someone say, "God looks at the heart".
No man can read the heart but God can.
Matthew 15:8 says that God knows those who are following Him closely.
He says, "I know My sheep, and I know them by name".
So, if you have chosen to follow the Lord in Spirit and in truth, you know the mind of Christ.
Let someone say, "The mind of Christ is meant for those who have the Spirit of Jesus Christ".
When you have the mind of Christ, you will know His will, you will know His way,
you will know His projects, His intention of everything that happens to you.
You will know God's point of view.
This leads us to our message this morning:
A MAN AFTER GOD'S OWN HEART
Say, "Neighbour, are you a man or woman after God's own heart?"
Ask yourself!
Let me take you to the Book of Acts 13:22.
I read from verse 22; it says:
"... when He had removed him, He raised up for them David as king,
to whom also He gave testimony and said, 'I have found David the son of Jesse,
a man after My own heart, who will do all My will.' "
Let someone say, "Who will do all My will. Who will do what is in My heart."
In the Book of 1 Samuel 16:6-22, David was considered the least suitable
according to man's standard.
But by God's recognition he was the right person, the right man - a man after God's own heart.
God looks at the heart.
The man of God says the heart refers to the inner man, the inner self, the real you, the man himself.
The heart is the seat of desire, will, emotions, wisdom, knowledge,
conscience and moral character.
Say, "Neighbour, are you a man after God's own heart?"
Ask your neighbour again.
If you are a man after God's own heart, you will feel what Jesus feels,
you will know what Jesus knows, you will see what has not yet been seen,
you will say what has not yet been said.
Ask David and he will tell you that if you are to be a man after God's own heart,
you have to go through God's processing.
Say, "Neighbour, if you are to be a man after God's own heart,
you have to go through God's processing."
What is that processing?
The Bible says anointing of God does not work through methods, plans, machinery;
it works through suitable men and women.
Anointing is the coming of the Holy Spirit into a person's life
to set him apart for God's special service and blessing.
Let someone say, "Anointing does not work through plans, methods or machinery;
it works through suitable men.
Ask your neighbour again, "Are you a man or woman God created for His glory?"
To be suitable means to receive heavenly acceptance.
To be fit, to be suitable, we must be reformed.
We have to be melted.
We have to be remoulded and we have to be filled.
We have to be melted first.
When you pass through the process of being melted,
you still need to be remoulded to fit Christ's standard.
And once you are remoulded as a vessel,
you still need to be filled with the Holy Spirit before you can be suitable for God's service.
Brethren, we need to be melted first.
Why?
Because the Bible says the glory of God cannot suffer corruption
and we all come a long way full of unrighteousness.
And for many of us, the process has not started yet - we are still in the raw stage:
a stage where we are too much of ourselves,
a stage where we are too much of where we are coming from.
That is why we need to go through the process.
If anyone refuses to go through this tiny step,
he will not be able to fulfill what God has designed him to achieve in His master plan.
Say, "Neighbour, we have to be melted."
The Bible says what God has designed you and me to achieve in His master plan
may not be situated in your father's house,
may not be situated in your country
but today many are still remaining in the warm arms of their parents, of their country.
They are afraid to venture into the cold arms of society
where the necessary lessons about life are learnt.
There is a battle, there is in the heart a struggle between the flesh and the spirit -
whether to believe or not, whether to believe God's calling or not.
That is why so many people today whom God has called to a position of leadership
still find themselves in the position of a follower.
Why?
Because they trust in man's majority, rather than God's authority.
They still wait for their parents to agree,
they still wait for their friends to agree,
they still wait for their community to agree
before they step out in boldness and take God at His Word.
Ask Apostle Paul and he will tell you in Galatians 1:15-17,
"When it pleased God to reveal His Son to me I did not consult any man".
It means if you have a sense of destiny, you will not look to people for direction;
you will step out in the direction of God's calling for your life.
Say, "Neighbour, we need to be melted."
In the process of being melted, there is the process of stripping.
Where God strips us of everything we counted dear, those things that hold us back,
so that we start believing God's authority rather than man's majority.
Our way of thinking, our way of life need to be reformed to suit Christ's purpose for our lives.
God melts us, God refines us through tribulation, trials, temptation.
The impact of those trials on ourselves separates us from impurities, pride,
arrogance, anger, resentment and causes the character of God to grow in our lives.
Let someone say, "True humility is total dependency upon God for everything."
Say, "Neighbour, are you melted? You need to be remoulded."
We need to be remoulded.
Why?
If you are to be God's person, an evangelist
you must go through the process of being remoulded.
Why?
Because we are concerned about building character.
Let someone say, "We are concerned about building character."
It is essential that we serve God with a willing heart.
A willing heart is a willing servant.
Let someone say, "A willing heart is a willing servant."
God is concerned about relationship - relationship forever.
That is why He said, "Seek first the Kingdom of God and His righteousness".
If you are a man after God's own heart, then you have a 'hugged' heart with God.
When you have your mind agree, your heart agree, in relationship with Jesus,
the Bible says that what is seen - whether you are healed or not,
whether you are blessed or not, whatever happens in your life, marriage, business -
that cannot affect your relationship with Jesus.
Say, "Neighbour, we need to be filled to be suitable for God's purpose."
Serving God without the Holy Spirit is serving a God we do not know.
Human spirit fails in doing so unless the Holy Spirit fills.
The Bible says the Holy Spirit is God's imperative of life.
He is a Giver of all life.
A new way of life, a new way of following God starts living in us
when Christ's Spirit starts dwelling in our hearts.
Let someone say, "A new way of life, a new way of following God
comes when God's Spirit starts living within us."
The man of God said it is the will of God for everyone to be filled with the Holy Spirit
because no one can speak on His behalf without His instinct.
In this world without faith, without hope, no one can stand for the truth,
no one can live right and stand for the truth without Him.
No one can obey the written Scriptures without the Spirit of God.
The Word and the Spirit are linked together.
We are made spiritual by living in the Word and the Word living in us.
Let me see your Bible.
Say, "This Word is the living presence of His divine power.
This Word is the living presence of Christ's divine power."
I am in it.
You are in it.
The picture of your future is in it.
Say, "My success is in it."
Thank You, Jesus!

The Bible says to live in the Spirit, keep in the Word,
live in the Word and let the Word live in you.
Be saturated with the Word of God
and you will find yourself overflowing with the Spirit.
The Word is Christ building Himself in us as it rules, dominates and sanctifies our spirit nature.
That's the Word of God.
When the Spirit of God came on the apostles on the day of Pentecost,
He enhanced their natural faculties,
He renewed their strength by filling them with power from on high
and then He confirmed their offices as apostles and people of God.
Say, "Neighbour, what is your dream?
Do you know what God has made you for?
It does not just happen.
There is a process."
God used hatred to melt Joseph's confidence in man.
God gave to Joseph a dream, a picture of his future greatness known to him alone.
But the moment Joseph shared his vision with others,
his brothers became hell-bent to frustrate, to destroy that dream.
But God used hatred, jealousy, resentment to release him into his divine future.
No matter what you are passing through, no matter His calling in your life,
God sometimes allows foolish things to come our way
to prepare us for the best, which is yet to come.
It is in the midst of trials, temptation, hatred
that God got Joseph to the place where He used him as a channel of blessing.
What is your dream?
What is your vision?
What is the promise of God for your life?
A dream is a roadmap.
Say, "My dream is my roadmap."
For that dream to be fulfilled, you must go through divine processing.
Our conduct, our character, our behaviour must be altered,
must be changed to suit Christ's standard.
Are you ready?
Are you ready to be melted?
Are you ready to be remoulded?
Are you ready to be filled?
The Bible says one's input determines one's output.
The way and manner we have been processing our lives
is what is working for or against us today.
No matter what you are experiencing today,
whatever stage you are in in this divine process,
the Bible says broken things become useful in the hands of God.
Let someone say, "Broken things become useful in the hands of God."
Finally brethren,
for every purpose of God in our lives to be fulfilled there must be a process
because Jesus Christ is concerned about our future.
Jesus does not want us to end up one day saying,
"I could have been... I should have been... I would have done..."
God loves us; God values us because we are the dwelling place of His Spirit -
our heart is God's temple.
Let someone say, "God values us.
God values me because I am the dwelling place of His Spirit."
Our heart is God's temple but the Holy Spirit cannot dwell in an unholy place.
That's why Jesus is busy building character now -
so that when we get to a place of destiny we may not crack, explode and lose it all.
Let someone say, "Jesus is busy building character now."
Let someone say, "I am a work of art."
My future, my destiny, my career, my life, God's goal for my life is being refashioned now.
Let someone say, "Now."
Thank You, Jesus!
