Here is Prophet T.B. Joshua's teacher, who taught him Primary 1, 2, 3 and 4.
We are joined today by Mrs. Margaret Tolani Adejumo, who was one of the first teachers of Prophet T.B. Joshua
back in his elementary educational days.
It is a great privilege to have her with us today.
First of all madam, could you introduce yourself to our viewers?
I am Mrs. Margaret Tolani Adejumo. I am a teacher by occupation.
I taught Prophet T.B. Joshua when he was in primary school.
When was this time? What year was this?
That was around 1974.
We would like to ask you what you remember about Prophet T.B. Joshua when he was in school?
What were the character or behavioural traits you remember seeing in him at that young age?
Prophet T.B. Joshua was a jovial pupil when I was teaching him.
He was not the class captain, but he acted as the class captain.
During the Scripture period, that is B.K. - which we call Bible Studies - he acted like a prophet.
When we finished talking about any topic in the Bible, he would further explain to the students what he understood
about the lesson I had taught them in Bible Knowledge (B.K.).
During that period, we called him 'small pastor' because he acted well.
People would say 'small pastor' because he was of small stature among his mates, but very active.
That was why people called him 'small pastor'.
He was very active, and he led them throughout the period he was in that class.
You have talked about him educationally, particularly in religious knowledge that he was very active in that subject.
Was he good in other subjects as well?
He was good in other subjects, but particularly in Bible Knowledge.
He was very bold and didn't tire of talking. If you asked him to explain something, he would explain it.
Whenever any of the pupils were causing problems, he would go and talk to them and try to make peace.
He would talk to them - "This is not good."
Every time he did this, he tried to talk to them from the Bible.
One day, when a mad man came to our school (he slept in the school compound), they saw him with a cutlass.
When the pupils came into the school, they were afraid of that man and no one could drive him away.
Even the teachers were trying to keep away from that mad man, telling the students to stay away from the man.
But T.B. Joshua went to him and people were crying, "No, don't go!"
But he said, "What? I will go to him."
So, he went to him and said, "Bring that cutlass" - he was commanding the man.
The mad man just gave the cutlass to him!
He then told the man to go, "Don't stay here", and he went away.
Did the man ever come back to the school to disturb you?
He didn't come back again.
- He never came back again?
- No.
This is an amazing story, and it reminds me of one of the teachings, where Prophet T.B. Joshua says that
David spoke the word of faith and the giant fell.
So, even at an early age, he was demonstrating the power in the word of faith.
At this incident, what was the reaction of the other students and the teachers?
I suspect he must have gained quite a reputation.
They were embarrassed because he was very small
and people that were taller than him couldn't come near the man.
They were afraid, thinking, "How could this small boy have the boldness to take that cutlass from the mad man?"
All the students and teachers were talking about him, "Only God knows what type of boy is this 'prophet'."
They were amazed and said, "This must be the Lord's doing".
Were there any other character traits that he displayed that made him different to the other pupils there?
During the time he was in the school, he had an interest in playing football.
If they were going to play anywhere, he would foretell whether his school or another school would win.
You are saying he would prophesy?
Yes, that "This school is going to win," or, "My school is going to win," and it would be so.
So, just as he would foretell or prophesy at that young age,
the outcome of the game before it happened would be as he said?
Yes and I knew he was going to be a great man.
You knew right from that young age that he was going to be great, and indeed today we can see the fruit of that.
I believe that must give you a lot of joy and happiness to see this man of God now
and know that you were one of the people who taught him and contributed to his life.
Yes.
