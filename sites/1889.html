HEADLINE: "As Ecuador struggles to contain the virus, economic anxiety also spreads"
HEADLINE: "The Sickness Is Trapping Ecuador Between Death And Debt"
HEADLINE: "Virus pandemic exposes inequality in Ecuador's Guayaquil"
Ecuador is one of the poorest countries in South America
and also one of the hardest hit by the virus pandemic.
T.B. Joshua and Emmanuel TV had helped this nation following the catastrophic earthquake of 2016,
and now both the national Government and local NGOs reached out to T.B. Joshua for support.
The Director of Cooperation of the Association of Ecuadorian Municipalities (AME)
sent an email to T.B. Joshua outlining the country's various needs that arose due to the pandemic.
She wrote, "Knowing your sense of humanity and solidarity, I am asking you to analyse the possibility,
through your institution, to support us with donations to meet the needs described above
so that the 221 Municipalities of Ecuador can better cope with the effects of the crisis
and reduce the impact in a timely manner.
Confident of your solidarity, we ratify the commitment and willingness of our institution
to comply with and support initiatives to mitigate the spread of the virus.
We are one world and now one fight.
For local solidarity, Mireille Vásconez, Director of Cooperation, Association of Ecuadorian Municipalities.
The request did not fall on deaf ears, and funded by T.B. Joshua and the Emmanuel TV Partners,
the local Emmanuel TV Team mobilised and gathered in Guayaquil
to arrange the purchase, packing and distribution of 1,500 much needed food and hygiene boxes
for the devastated neighbouring community of Duran.
