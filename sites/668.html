STANDARD FOR LIFE
My name is Josephine Iweke.
I am from Imo State, residing in Abuja.
I am a teacher, teaching at a junior
secondary school.
I was in the first service when the man
of God came to me and said,
"Sister, you are homeless. What
happened to your former house?"
I got up and said, "It's true, Man of
God. It was burnt by neighbours".
After they burned it, the people that I am working with in the educational department
said that my life was not safe in that village.
So they transferred me to a town where I began to work in the school there.
The cost of living inside town was very difficult for me, with what I was passing through.
I asked my principal if she could get me a place to sleep at.
She said she did not have but asked if I could manage to sleep in the classroom after school.
I told her that I could manage.
After school, I would bring out my things where I had hidden them,
sweep the floor and sleep on a mat somebody had given me.
I would sleep on a mat next to the security man.
First thing in the morning, I would fetch water from the borehole, bathe and dress up.
Other teachers thought that I used to come to school very early.
I used to be punctual.
They were seeing me but didn't even know what had happened.
My principal was shedding tears. She said that she didn't want anybody to know
that I was staying there as a female teacher, for my safety.
That is how I became homeless in Abuja, up till now.
When we closed for this vacation, I went home to see my parents.
My mother then started crying because the case was in court.

Then I came here with my parents.
The man of God prophesied to me that I was homeless. Up till now, I am homeless.
Everything in my bedroom got burnt. I had two rooms.
The other room had a plastic chair and a small television.
