T.B. JOSHUA
QUESTION:
A well-known minister of God recently renounced Christianity because he said
there were no more miracles happening.
Man of God, what can you say about this?
ANSWER:
If the age of miracles is passed, no one could be born-again.
Anyone who claims that the age of miracles has passed is not yet born-again -
I mean, he is not a Christian, believer, pastor, evangelist.
Indeed, the greatest miracle is to be born-again!
For you to be born-again, you have to be a product of miracles.
You cannot say there are no miracles when you are a product of miracles!
Is it a miracle of healing for an affliction that you are referring to?
Believe me, that is a lesser miracle!
I repeat: The greatest miracle is to be born-again.
You cannot deny miracles since you are a product of miracles -
if truly you are born-again.
If unbelievers say this, we can understand - because they can also say Jesus is not the Lord.
Are we referring to unbelievers or believers?
If you are not a Christian, there is no way you can understand the depth,
the insight of the nature of God.
You may see, hear and learn but not practise.
You can master and quote the chapters and verses of the Bible for many years,
hear thousands of sermons and read many Christian books but still wear spiritual diapers.
For God to hear and answer prayer - whether the petition is for a postage stamp,
or for you to sleep and wake up or for the healing of someone crippled - it is a miracle.
If your prayer brings an answer, that answer is a miracle!
If there are no miracles, then there is no reason for faith.
God cannot answer prayer without miracle.
TT Robert Begier 29/01/2020
